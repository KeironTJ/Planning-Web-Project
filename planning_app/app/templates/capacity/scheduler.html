How Backward Scheduling Works
  1. Starts from the order's ERP due date.
  2. Walks backwards through routing stages (highest sequence first).
  3. For each stage, subtracts the lead time in working days (from your Labour Plan calendar). Falls back to Mon–Thu if no calendar data.
  4. Departments in the same stage (same sequence order) run in parallel — the stage uses the longest lead time among them.
  5. Departments not in the routing template are placed at the end and use their department default lead time.
  6. Overdue orders (floor date ≥ due date) are scheduled forward from the floor date instead.
